Democracy in UNCC

Basic Info about the Space

Union

The Popp Martin Student Union on UNC Charlotte's campus is home to a variety of dining options, outside of the dining hall “Social 704”. These dining options include Bojangles, Shake Smart, Wendy’s, Starbucks, Erbert and Gerbert’s, and Amazon Just Walk Out (JWO).

Physical Layout

The main doors of the student union open to a large, cylindrical open floor plan that allows a student or visitor to choose where they would like to go. Starbucks is just to the left of the main entrance. Walking straight through the lounge area found in the heart of the student union will lead you to Union Square. Union Square is home to Bojangles, Wendy’s, Shake Smart, and Amazon JWO. Erbert and Gerbert’s sandwich and soup shop can be found inside the 49er Fresh, just to the right of Norm’s Lounge.

Social Behavior

The Popp Martin Student Union is the closest dining option to the Student Recreation area (UREC) and north campus dormitories. This combination of key student areas on campus leads to the Popp Martin Student Union not only being a great hub for dining, but also a common lounging area used by students. When entering the Popp Martin Student Union, you can often find students meeting up to enjoy the dining options, studying in the lounge or just hanging out. Whether it is a hangout spot or a study area, students from all backgrounds frequently use this area.

Accessibility and inclusion

Students from all backgrounds frequently use the Popp Martin Student Union. It is also a key area shown during campus tours for incoming freshmen and parents. These dining options are not only available to students, but also to any visitors to the University. These non-dining options are catered to any person to use and do not discriminate based on enrollment or background.

Technology Presents

One method for ordering food at these non-dining hall options is kiosks spread out over the Union Square. Students and visitors are able to use card or Niner accounts pre-loaded with money to pay for these food options. Ordering on the kiosks sends the food order to the respective food option, where the person then waits for the food to be prepared. This makes ordering food at these options efficient due to the high amounts of orders these options see on a daily basis. A second method of ordering food includes using the dine on campus app that students are recommended to download. This method allows students to order food from anywhere on campus and walk to the Popp Martin Student Union to pick up their food. The final option is to use the Starship app if you live on campus in one of the dormitories. Starship is an automated food delivery system that uses delivery robots to carry the food to designated drop-off locations chosen by the students on the app. The Amazon JWO is a new area in the Popp Martin Student Union. This convenience area allows a person to swipe in using their card or student account, pick up their items, and walk out of the store. Whatever is taken from this market is then charged to whatever method a person swipes in with.

Rules and structures

There are no formal rules when using these non-dining hall options on campus. They are meant to be accessible to any student or visitor and are recommended for any person to use on the UNC Charlotte campus. The only informal rules are based on the ordering method and picking up your food in a timely manner.

Thoughtful Cup

Thoughtful cup is a coffee shop located in the Fretwell building. It serves as a place for students to grab a drink or snack, sit down to study, meet friends, or take a break between classes.

Physical Layout

Although the space is compact, there is plenty of space for students to sit. There are front and rear windows, chairs and tables facing the wall on both sides, and a large table in the center that can seat about eight people. There is also a small coffee table with four floor chairs that sits lower to the ground. At the front entrance, tucked into a corner, is the actual coffee shop and cashier area with a pickup station.There is enough room to move comfortably between the front and table sections. Both individual and group seating are available, and the space connects to both indoor and outdoor seating.

Social Behavior

Students use the space both alone and in groups. Some are studying quietly, while others are talking with friends at a normal volume. The music plays at a respectful volume, which keeps the atmosphere peaceful. Some students stop by only to grab coffee, while others stay longer and use their laptop and phones. Most employee and student interactions happen during the ordering process, and the interactions are polite and friendly.

Acessibility and Inclusion

The coffee shop's front entrance is spacious enough to accommodate guests entering or leaving. There is enough room for wheelchair users, and the chairs around the walls and in the center can be moved if needed.The overall environment feels welcoming as soon as you walk in. The pricing is also fairly accessible, with drinks and snacks ranging from $1 to $10, and they accept cards and declining balance, which is convenient to students.

Technology Present

There are plenty of outlets for charging laptops and phones. Free Wi-Fi is available. The shop uses a register and card reader system, and students can also order online through the Niner Diners app. A printer is also available nearby, which adds to the space's usefulness for students.

Formal rules include store hours, menu board, the ordering line, pickup process, and any posted signs. Informal rules come from student behavior, such as keeping noise low, sharing tables respectfully, and understanding when to stay for long study sessions or just making a quick stop

Definition of Democracy

Democracy is a participatory and communicative process where a collective of people shape decisions about the meaning and aspects of public life. In a perfect world this would have equal inclusion within an exclusive people where there are shared principles. However in practice it is never perfect and ever evolving with new social spaces, shifting power dynamics, and technology.
